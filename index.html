§ 03 / The Manifesto

WE BELIEVE.

Re-issued annually
From the editor's desk

That a brand is a logo sentence. That copy is the part of design that is read out loud. That tone is the new typography. That the right word, set in the right place, on a Tuesday afternoon, can do the work of a media buy. That if it can be deleted, it should be. That the best writing sounds like a person you would trust to feed your dog. We are not here to fill space. We are here to fill the page with the smallest possible number of words that could only have come from you.

— Maren Westcott, founder

§ 04 / How we work

THE METHOD.

Four weeks · sometimes three
01

Listen,
at length.

Two long calls. We read every email your founder ever wrote. We ask what you wish you sounded like, and what you do not.

02

Argue,
on paper.

A first draft of voice, tone, and three sample pieces. Marked up by us, then by you. We don't write to please; we write to be true.

03

Refine,
in red pen.

Second pass. Cuts. New cuts. A line you forgot you wanted to read. The version that survives this round is usually the version that ships.

04

Hand it
over.

Final assets, a voice guideline your team will actually open, and a 30-day window where we answer texts about commas.
